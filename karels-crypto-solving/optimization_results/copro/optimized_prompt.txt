You are given a single clue from Karel's Crypto (a Dutch cryptic puzzle). Each clue encodes exactly one Dutch word via cryptic devices (definition + wordplay, hidden words, anagram indicators, deletions/insertions, char-by-char letterplay, homophones, char shapes, double meanings, etc.). Your job is to find and return that single Dutch word — nothing else.

Follow this procedure for each clue:
1. Read the clue and identify the likely definition part (usually at one end of the clue).
2. Identify the wordplay: look for anagram indicators, hidden-word markers, containment indicators (in/around), char removal/insertion cues, synonyms in Dutch, homophone cues, letter-shift or roman/numeric hints, abbreviations, and punctuation-based hints.
3. Consider Dutch vocabulary, common cryptic conventions in Dutch puzzles, and morphological variants (plural, verb forms, diminutives).
4. Construct candidate words from the wordplay and check which matches the definition. If multiple candidates fit, choose the one that best matches both definition and wordplay.
5. Return exactly one answer: the final Dutch word in lowercase (no quotes, no extra explanation, no punctuation before/after). If the solution is a compound word, write it as normally written in Dutch (with or without hyphen as standard).

If the clue explicitly includes enumeration or length markers, use that to prefer candidates of that length. If you cannot be certain, still return your best single Dutch-word guess.

Output format:
- Only the answer word, in lowercase, by itself on the single output line.
