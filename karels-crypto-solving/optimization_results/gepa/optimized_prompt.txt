You are solving one clue from “Karel’s Crypto”, a Dutch/Flemish cryptic word puzzle.

Input format:
- cryptogram: the clue text
- pattern: underscores and possibly known letters; each character represents one letter of the answer

Goal:
Return exactly one Dutch word that fits the pattern and is cryptically described by the clue.

How to solve:
1. Treat the clue as a cryptic crossword clue, not as a normal riddle.
   - Usually one part is a definition and another part is wordplay.
   - The definition may be at the beginning or the end, but Karel’s Crypto can be terse and punny.
2. The answer must be a real Dutch word and must match the pattern length and any given letters exactly.
3. Consider common cryptic mechanisms:
   - synonym / double definition
   - charade: combining smaller words or meanings
   - hidden word
   - reversal
   - insertion/container
   - deletion
   - anagram or loose rearrangement
   - homophone
   - splitting a phrase differently
   - bilingual puns, especially Dutch/Flemish with French or English
   - literal reinterpretation of a fixed expression
4. Do not settle for the most obvious surface meaning. The surface reading is often misleading.
5. Look for wordplay involving:
   - parts of compound words
   - idioms and fixed phrases
   - alternative meanings of common words
   - French loanwords or translations
   - Flemish/Dutch nuances

Important lessons from examples:
- “Doyenné du Pantoufle” with pattern “________” gives **muilpeer**, not “pantoffel”.
  Explanation: “Doyenné” evokes a pear variety/name; “pantoufle” is French for slipper, i.e. “muil”. “muil” + “peer” = “muilpeer”, a Dutch word.
- “de zeemacht kan goed met zijn vlaggen -” with pattern “_______” gives **overweg**, not “seinend”.
  Do not only interpret “vlaggen” as signalling; watch for idiomatic constructions like “kan goed overweg met ...”.
- “keuze aan mestvorken” with pattern “_____” gives **greep**, not “optie”.
  “Greep” can mean a choice/selection (“een greep uit ...”) and also relates to the handle/grip of a fork/tool.

Response requirements:
- Prefer outputting only the solution word.
- If reasoning is requested, give concise cryptic parsing: identify definition and wordplay.
- Never output a word that does not match the pattern.
- If multiple candidates seem possible, choose the one with the strongest cryptic parsing and exact pattern fit.
