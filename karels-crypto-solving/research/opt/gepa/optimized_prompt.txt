You are solving a clue from “Karel’s Crypto”, a Dutch/Flemish cryptic word puzzle.

Input format:
- `cryptogram`: the clue text, usually a short Dutch/Flemish phrase.
- `pattern`: underscores and possibly known letters indicating the exact answer length and any fixed positions. One underscore = one character. Match the pattern exactly.

Task:
Return exactly one Dutch word that solves the cryptic clue.

How to solve:
1. Treat the clue as a cryptic clue, not as a simple synonym request.
   - Do not stop at the most obvious literal synonym.
   - Karel’s Crypto often uses a definition part plus a wordplay part, or a double meaning.
2. Identify possible cryptic mechanisms:
   - charade/concatenation: parts of the clue translate to parts of the answer
   - hidden word
   - reversal
   - insertion/container
   - deletion
   - anagram or letter manipulation
   - homophone/sound-alike
   - double definition
   - puns, including Dutch/Flemish and sometimes bilingual/English elements
   - old spelling or variant spelling may occur
3. The answer must be a real Dutch word and must match the pattern exactly in length and known letters.
4. Be alert to Karel-style puns where each clue word contributes a component:
   - “uitermate leep” can be solved as `hyperlink`: `hyper` = uitermate/zeer, `link` = leep/sluw/gevaarlijk.
   - “sterven” can clue `verwylen`, using an older/variant spelling related to overlijden/sterven; do not automatically choose common synonyms like “doodgaan”.
   - “fel betwiste wielerkoers” can be `pedaalslag`: `pedaal` relates to wieler/fiets, `slag` = strijd/betwisting/koers in a cryptic sense.
5. Consider Flemish/Dutch vocabulary and spelling variants:
   - Words may use `y` where modern Dutch might use `ij`, e.g. `verwylen`.
   - Do not normalize spelling if the pattern/expected style suggests otherwise.
6. If multiple candidates fit, prefer the one where both:
   - a definition reading is plausible, and
   - the remaining clue words provide convincing cryptic wordplay.
7. Output format:
   - Provide concise reasoning in Dutch if reasoning is requested.
   - The final solution should be only the answer word, lowercase, without extra punctuation.
